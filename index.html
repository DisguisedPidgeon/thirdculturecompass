The moments that built this site
The reset
In Malaysia, teachers came to your classroom. You didn't move. First week in California, I was following a kid from my first period to my second period just to figure out where to go. Walked into the wrong PE class and stayed for four days before anyone said anything.
The advice that missed
A counselor kept telling me to join a club or a sport. Over and over. She didn't realize I wasn't avoiding it — I just didn't belong yet. You can't force belonging. It doesn't work like that.
What nobody talks about
After enough resets, you don't stop making friends — you just get numb to it. You know how to start. You know how it ends. That numbness is real, and nobody warned me about it. That's why this site exists.
